Rayleigh-Ritz Method

A numerical method to approximate deflections and buckling loads by minimizing total potential energy.

1. Deflection Analysis

Assume a shape function v(x) = Σ ci φi(x) that satisfies boundary conditions.

Total Potential Energy (Π):
Π = U - W

Strain Energy (U): ∫ (EI/2) (v'')² dx
External Work (W): ∫ q v dx
The Solution: Differentiate Π with respect to each coefficient (ci) and set to zero:
∂Π / ∂ci = 0

2. Buckling Analysis (Pcr)

For a column under axial load P, buckling occurs when the internal strain energy equals the potential of the external load.

Bending Energy (U): ∫ (EI/2) (v'')² dx
Load Potential (Ve): ∫ (P/2) (v')² dx
Critical Load Formula:
Pcr = [ ∫ EI (v'')² dx ] / [ ∫ (v')² dx ]

Key Reminders for Rauf:

  • Degrees: For a point load, v'' is linear (1st degree), so v must be 3rd degree.
  • Symmetry: For symmetric loads, use symmetric functions (even powers or sines) to save calculation time.
  • Boundary Conditions: The trial function must satisfy essential BCs (displacement and slope at supports).
