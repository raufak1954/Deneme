Technical Assumptions:
• Method: Hamilton Standard Generalized Method (Static, J=0).
• TAF Adjustment: Total Activity Factor = AF × Blades (Normalized to 200 TAF ref).
• CLi Adjustment: Scaled relative to an integrated design lift coefficient of 0.5.
• P_abs (Absorbed Power): Aerodynamic power consumed by the propeller.
• SHP (Shaft Horsepower): Engine power delivered to the gearbox (SHP = P_abs / Efficiency).
• Atmosphere: ISA Standard Density at specified altitude.
Model Airplane Note:
For model airplanes, lower the Design Lift (CLi) to 0.3–0.5 and increase the Activity Factor (AF) to 120–150. Due to low Reynolds numbers, these blades cannot sustain the high lift coefficients of full-scale aircraft. For electric motors, set Mechanical Efficiency to 80% to account for ESC and motor heat losses.
